	Petitions were presented against the opening of public houses on Sunday, by Mr. J R Yorke, from Stow-on-the-Wold; by Colonel Kingscote, from the Gloucestershire Law Society, against the Lords’ amendments to the Justices’ Clerks Bill, and by Mr. Forsyth, from Charles Bradlaugh and Annie Besant, for a measure to make the law clear and distinct as to liberty of discussion on the question of over-population and its remedies.
