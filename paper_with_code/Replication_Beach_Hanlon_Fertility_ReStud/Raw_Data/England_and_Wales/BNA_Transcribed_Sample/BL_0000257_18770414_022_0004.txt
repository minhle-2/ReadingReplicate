ARREST OF MR CHARLES BRADLAIGH.—Two well-known persons, Mr. Charles Bradlaugh and Mrs. Annie Besant, were charged at the Guildhall Police Court on Thursday with publishing an alleged immoral pamphlet, called The Fruits of Philosophy. They were arrested on a warrant, according to the usual custom in can of this kind, and Mr. Bradlaugh undertook the defence of himself and his co-prisoner. A publisher named Watts had previously been summoned before the Court for selling copies of the book, and he had then agreed to stop the publication. Mr. Bradlaugh, who contends that the book is not indecent, republished it? Having given notice to the police authorities of his intention to do so. A remand until the 17th inst. was ordered, and bait was taken.
