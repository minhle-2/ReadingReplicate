       On Tuesday Mrs. Besant continued her address, arguing that as the natural checks in population, such as war famine and disease were removed, it was necessary that scientific checks should be substituted. Mrs. Besant was proceeding to treat of the Darwinian doctrine of the survival of the fittest, when Lord Chief Justice Cockburn remarked it would be worthy Mr. Darwin’s consideration, whether the result of the struggle for existence was not twofold, namely, that beneath the small numbers of highly gifted race, a deteriorated mans would yet be engendered. Mrs. Besant having continued her arguments in the case was followed by Mr. Bradlaugh, who had not concluded his address when the court rose.
